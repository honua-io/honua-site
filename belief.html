// the honua belief

Geospatial is
ordinary modern software.

Your geometry is just another (very complex) column in the database you already run, and spatial analysis is just analytics. The expertise is real — projections, predicates, topology are hard-won craft — but the separate universe of servers, licenses, and toolchains built around it never needed to exist. Absorbing that complexity is the software's job, so everything you touch stays simple.

Five things we hold to be true.

01 · Cost scales with workload, not adoption

Infrastructure you already know how to price, and a flat license. No credits, no named users, no per-core arithmetic. Adding your thousandth user costs nothing.
