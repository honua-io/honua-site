Priced like infrastructure.
Because it is infrastructure.

We publish the model so you can verify it is predictable before you ever talk to us. We are in early access, so the numbers are set in a short conversation — and the model below is the contract shape those numbers land in.

The model in one paragraph

Honua is licensed per node, annually — a node is a serving instance, counted by capacity, not by processor cores. You buy a capacity band that covers your everyday fleet, and your variable cost is your own cloud infrastructure, which you already know how to price. Users are unlimited. Always.

What the model guarantees

  • Cost scales with workload, not adoption. Adding your thousandth user costs nothing. No named users, no credits, no per-core arithmetic.
  • One flat annual number. Your invoice does not vary within the term. Autoscaling inside your band is free, including short bursts above it.
  • Growth is forward-only. If you sustainably outgrow your band, you pay the pro-rated difference to the next one from that day forward — never backward. No true-ups, ever.
  • You can size down at renewal. If your footprint shrinks, so does your price. We advertise this because almost nobody else offers it.
  • Emergency surge always works. When you declare an emergency, the capacity ceiling lifts entirely — your operations dashboard can scale twenty-fold during the storm without a licensing conversation. Surge coverage with a response SLA is available for organizations whose mission is emergency operations.
  • There are no audits. The license enforces its own capacity band, and you see the same meter we do. The dashboard is the audit.
  • Non-production is free. Dev, test, staging, and cold-standby DR capacity don't count.
  • Topology-neutral. VMs, Kubernetes, or serverless — licensed by concurrent serving capacity, so re-architecting never reprices you.

Editions

  • Community — free, self-hosted, production-capable, under ELv2. The full protocol surface with no license key. This is the official path for small organizations and evaluation; see how the open core works.
  • Pro — per-node annual capacity bands for production deployments.
  • Enterprise — Pro plus governance: SSO, RBAC, audit, compliance posture, and premium support options.

Support is a separate, visible line item — never silently bundled into the software price.

Buy through your cloud marketplace

Standard cloud deployments transact through AWS Marketplace (Azure following) as flat annual contracts — which means the purchase draws down your existing committed cloud spend and rides the marketplace standard contract. For public-sector buyers, that is a PO against a vehicle you already have, not a six-month legal negotiation. Negotiated terms, multi-year agreements, and larger capacity run as marketplace private offers.

What we will never do

  • No per-user or named-user pricing
  • No per-core pricing
  • No per-request, per-GB, or metered-usage invoices
  • No retroactive repricing or true-ups
  • No license audits
  • No throttling of running workloads — the license sizes your fleet; it never touches a request

Get a number

Early-access pricing is set per deployment in a short conversation: tell us your current GIS line item and your rough footprint, and you'll get a flat annual quote in the shape above. If you're coming from ArcGIS Server, bring your renewal date — that's usually the right moment to compare.
