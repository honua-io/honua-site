Open Core

Pricing that scales with infrastructure and delivery work, not with seat counts.

Honua separates the platform, support, and migration work so teams can understand what they are buying. The core idea is simple: users and downstream systems should not trigger a new license purchase every time adoption grows.

What Customers Usually Care About

No seat tax, no protocol tax, and no surprise split between “basic” and “real” product.

No Seat Pricing

Add users, field teams, and downstream systems without per-user licensing growth.

Honua is designed for infrastructure-style adoption, where more usage should mean more value, not a negotiation every time another group needs access.

No Protocol Gating

The runtime surface is not split into hidden add-ons.

Customers should not have to unlock compatibility, standards, and modern interfaces as separate products.

Services Are Separate

Migration work is scoped like delivery work, not bundled into vague platform pricing.

That gives teams a cleaner buying path when the software and the rollout effort are both part of the conversation.
