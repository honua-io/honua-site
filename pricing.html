Open Core

Pricing that scales with infrastructure and delivery work, not with seat counts.

Honua separates the platform, support, and migration work so teams can understand what they are buying. The core idea is simple: users and downstream systems should not trigger a new license purchase every time adoption grows.

What Customers Usually Care About

No seat tax, no protocol tax, and no surprise split between “basic” and “real” product.

No Seat Pricing

Add users, field teams, and downstream systems without per-user licensing growth.

Honua is designed for infrastructure-style adoption, where more usage should mean more value, not a negotiation every time another group needs access.

No Protocol Gating

The runtime surface is not split into hidden add-ons.

Customers should not have to unlock compatibility, standards, and modern interfaces as separate products.

Services Are Separate

Migration work is scoped as a delivery engagement, not hidden inside an opaque platform fee.

That makes procurement clearer when teams need both the software and help with the rollout.

How Commercial Work Gets Structured

Platform, support, and migration help are different things.

Platform Terms

Commercial packaging for production use.

This covers the product agreement for teams that are moving from evaluation to production deployment.

Support + Services

Operational coverage and migration help when teams need it.

Use support tiers for runtime operations and scoped services when the rollout itself needs planning, delivery work, or proof points.

If your team is already running live ArcGIS or GeoServer workloads, the assessment is the best place to start because it defines what your migration actually involves, separate from general product evaluation.
