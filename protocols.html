Protocols

Compatibility gets teams in. gRPC and MCP show what is actually new.

Honua brings GeoServices REST, OGC API, OData, gRPC, MCP, and cloud-native tiles together in one runtime. Compatibility explains migration. gRPC and MCP explain why the platform is modern.

Innovation Layer

The real protocol differentiation is here.

gRPC

Application and mobile performance rail

`gRPC` is not just another protocol checkbox. It is the binary transport that makes app integrations, MAUI field workflows, and high-throughput client paths feel substantially different from pure REST-era GIS stacks.

  • Strong fit for typed SDKs and mobile field clients.
  • Unary support belongs in the open protocol story.
  • Streaming is the commercial scale path, not the compatibility wedge.

MCP

Agent-native geospatial access rail

`MCP` is the surface that makes Honua legible to coding agents, copilots, and analyst assistants. It turns service discovery, schema inspection, and filtered queries into tool-safe workflows instead of ad hoc scraping.

  • Discovery and query workflows for AI assistants.
  • Direct bridge from geospatial systems into agent ecosystems.
  • A meaningful differentiation point versus legacy GIS stacks.

Coverage

OData v4

Spatial data in business systems

gRPC

Binary transport for high-throughput paths

gRPC is the modern app and mobile rail. Unary support is part of the open surface. Streaming is the scale and performance unlock that differentiates paid runtime tiers.

MCP

Agent-native access

MCP gives AI assistants and agents a safe path to discovery, schema inspection, and query workflows on top of Honua data. It is a platform innovation, not a footnote.

Tiles and Classic OGC

MVT, WMS, and WMTS

Vector tiles, WMS 1.3, and WMTS 1.0 keep the platform useful across mapping stacks, older integrations, and standards-heavy environments.
